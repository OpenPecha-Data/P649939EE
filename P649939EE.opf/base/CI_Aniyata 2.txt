2. Whatever monk should sit down with a woman, one with the other,in secret, on a concealed
seat not suitable to have sexual intercourse.lv” and if a trustworthy upasika should accuse
[him] of one or another of two dharmas: [either] with a saṃghāvaśeṣa or pāyāntika dharma,
that monk, because of admitting that he was so seated, [should be dealt with] according
to one or another of two dharmas: [either] a saṃghāvaśeṣa or pāyāntika dharma; or by whichever
dharma that trustworthy upasika should accuse that monk. So should that monk be dealt
with by this or that dharma. This is an aniyata.
